Are the probabilities honest?

Each dot is a group of games at a similar predicted probability. Closer to the dashed line = the stated probability matched reality.

Reading: the dots hug the diagonal within a few points, so when the model says “48% chance of a run,” about 48% of those games have one. That's what “calibrated” means — the number can be trusted at face value, even though the model can't yet separate winners from losers.

How to read this dashboard

NRFI vs. YRFI

NRFI = No Runs First Inning. YRFI = Yes Runs First Inning. The bars show the model's probability of each.

Why not 90% accuracy?

Whether a run scores in one inning is nearly a coin flip league-wide (~50%). Any honest model wins by small, consistent margins — not blowout accuracy.

Where it stands

Built on 19,318 fully-featured games with leak-proof, pre-game-only inputs. Calibrated today; a real edge would come from confirmed lineups, umpires, and Statcast — the roadmap.
